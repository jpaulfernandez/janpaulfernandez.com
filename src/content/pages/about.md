I'm a tech leader from the Philippines.

I started as a Systems Analyst at Philippine Savings Bank, which gave me foundational knowledge and durable skills school never taught me. From there I moved to RightCrowd, an Australian-based physical identity and access management platform — though Australian hours weren't for me.

Then I joined Rappler during the height of attacks on press freedom. I came in as a frontend developer and got thrown into the fire, eventually becoming Head of Tech Operations. We built PHVote, Rappler's platform for monitoring elections, and I covered both the 2019 senatorial and 2022 presidential races. I also led the migration of the old site to a new CMS, enabling things like ontology and a knowledge graph. Rappler is where I matured — and where I took on the advocacy of using tech for good.

I think of technology as a gun. The trouble is that plenty of people who know how to wield it use it for harm.

Right now my interest is AI. It's a deeply nuanced topic — honestly, part of me hates it — but it's here, and we have to take part in making sure it's used for good, not to oppress people further.

I'm currently taking my Master's in Innovation and Business at the Asian Institute of Management.
